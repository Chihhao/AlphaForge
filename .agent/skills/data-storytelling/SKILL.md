# Data Storytelling

Transforming complex data into clear, persuasive narratives that drive decision-making.

## Use this skill when

- Presenting analytics to stakeholders
- Creating data-driven reports or articles
- Designing interactive dashboards
- Explaining complex trends or patterns
- Building persuasive arguments backed by data

## Do not use this skill when

- The task is pure data engineering or cleaning
- Accurate reporting is secondary to narrative (never sacrifice truth)
- Data privacy prevents sharing specific details

## Instructions

- Identify the core message and the target audience.
- Select the most effective visualization for the data type.
- Use the "Three Pillars" (Data, Visuals, Narrative) to structure the story.
- Apply progressive reveal techniques for complex information sets.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Story Structure
- **Beginning**: Define the context, current state, and the conflict/problem.
- **Middle**: Present the evidence (data), identify drivers, and explore nuances.
- **End**: Provide a clear resolution, actionable insights, and "what's next."

### 2. Narrative Arc
- **The Hook**: Grab attention with a surprising or critical data point.
- **The Build**: Layers of data that add depth to the initial hook.
- **The Peak**: The most significant finding or logical conclusion.
- **The Call to Action**: What should the audience do with this information?

### 3. Three Pillars
- **Data**: Accurate, clean, and relevant figures.
- **Visuals**: Charts, color-coding, and layout that emphasize the points.
- **Narrative**: Words that connect the dots and provide "The Why."

## Story Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Problem-Solution Story
1. Identify a negative trend or bottleneck in the data.
2. Show the impact (financial, performance, user-wise).
3. Point to the data that reveals the potential fix.
4. Predict the outcome of the solution.

### Framework 2: The Trend Story
1. Show historical progression of a metric.
2. Identify the "Inflection Point" where something changed.
3. Use data to explain what caused the pivot.
4. Forecast future trajectory.

### Framework 3: The Comparison Story
1. Show the delta between Group A and Group B (or Company vs. Industry).
2. Highlight why one is overperforming or underperforming.
3. Extract generalizable lessons from the high performer.

## Visualization Techniques

### Technique 1: Progressive Reveal
Start with a high-level aggregate and "drill down" as the narrative progresses. Don't dump a 20-column table on the user immediately.

### Technique 2: Contrast and Compare
Use color explicitly (e.g., Gold for keywords, Red for warnings/risk) to create instant cognitive recognition.

### Technique 3: Annotation and Highlight
Use text labels directly on charts to explain *what* the user is looking at in the context of the story (e.g., "KD Golden Cross here").
